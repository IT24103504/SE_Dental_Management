Common Dental Disorders

Understanding and treating various dental conditions

Tooth Decay & Cavities

Dental caries or tooth decay occurs when bacteria form a film (plaque) on teeth and produce acid that erodes tooth enamel. Early signs include dark spots and sensitivity.

  • Regular brushing & flossing
  • Fluoride treatments
  • Dental sealants

Periodontal Disease

Gum disease begins with gingivitis and can progress to periodontitis. Symptoms include bleeding gums, recession, and loose teeth if left untreated.

  • Professional cleaning
  • Scaling & root planing
  • Antibiotic treatment

Tooth Sensitivity

Sensitivity occurs when tooth enamel wears down or gums recede, exposing dentin. Common triggers include hot, cold, sweet, and acidic substances.

  • Desensitizing toothpaste
  • Fluoride treatments
  • Bonding procedures

Malocclusion

Misaligned teeth or incorrect bite relationships can cause difficulty in chewing, speaking, and maintaining oral hygiene. May lead to TMJ disorders.

  • Orthodontic braces
  • Clear aligners
  • Dental surgery

Dental Abscess

A severe infection that creates a pocket of pus in tooth or gum tissue. Causes severe pain, swelling, and fever. Requires immediate treatment.

  • Root canal therapy
  • Antibiotics
  • Drainage procedure

Dental Trauma

Injuries to teeth and surrounding tissues from accidents or impacts. Can include chips, cracks, displacement, or complete tooth loss.

  • Emergency care
  • Dental bonding
  • Crown placement
